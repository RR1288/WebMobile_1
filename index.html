Reality

The concepts of "reality" and "perception" have been subjects of philosophical contemplation for centuries, offering unique insights into the human understanding of the world. This essay delves into the perspectives of three prominent philosophers, namely Plato, René Descartes, and David Chalmers, shedding light on their distinct views on reality and perception. Furthermore, in the digital age, the dissemination of fake news and the influence of bots have added complexity to the interplay between these concepts. This essay explores how perception can be manipulated in the context of fake news and bots, with a particular emphasis on the critical factors involved.

Plato

Plato's Platonic Realism posited that reality consists of eternal and unchanging forms or ideas, existing beyond the physical world. He expressed skepticism about the reliability of sensory perception and emphasized the role of reason in accessing the world of unchanging forms.

Rene Descartes

Descartes introduced Cartesian Dualism, which delineated a sharp distinction between the mind and the body. He questioned the reliability of sensory perception and asserted the indubitable knowledge of the thinking self through the famous phrase "I think, therefore I am."

David Chalmers

Chalmers, known for his work in the philosophy of mind, contemplated the "hard problem of consciousness" and the challenge of understanding how physical processes in the brain give rise to subjective experience. He emphasized the role of perception in shaping our subjective reality. (Chatfield, 2022)
