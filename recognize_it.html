How to recognize it?

In today's digital age, the ability to critically assess information is crucial to avoid falling prey to misinformation and fake news. By considering these key factors, you can better equip yourself to identify and combat the spread of false information. (Kiely & Robertson, 2016)

1. Consider the source

  • Verify the credibility of the source.
  • Be cautious of websites with suspicious URLs and a lack of transparency about their mission and location.
  • Check fact-checking websites like Snopes for lists of known fake news websites.

2. Read Beyond the Headline

  • Don't be swayed by sensational headlines; read the full article.
  • Fake news may contain satirical elements and outlandish claims within the text.
  • Pay attention to the content to spot telltale signs of deception.

3. Check the Author and Supporting Sources

  • Scrutinize the author's identity and qualifications.
  • Be wary of fictitious bylines or authors with questionable credentials.
  • Investigate the cited sources to ensure they actually support the claims made.

4. Check the Date

  • Verify the publication date to distinguish between current and outdated stories.
  • False stories may twist real events to suit their narrative, so look for timely and accurate information.
  • Be cautious of articles that take old news and repackage it as current.

5. Satire vs. Reality

  • Differentiate between satire and genuine news.
  • Satire is typically labelled as such and may include humour.
  • Familiarize yourself with satirical writers and publications.
