Fake News

What is it?

Fake News entails misleading or entirely false information, capitalizes on the vulnerability of human perception. It often imitates traditional news formats, making it appear credible, and incorporates fabricated details loosely connected to actual events. Fake news strategically employs deceptive titles, images, and content to grab readers' attention and convince them of false narratives.

The primary goals of disseminating fake news are to boost engagement, such as clicks and shares, leading to increased advertising revenue and the potential to sway ideological beliefs. (Baptista & Gradim, 2020)

Beauvais (2022) defines fake news as “a type of online disinformation, with totally or partially false content, created intentionally to deceive and/or manipulate a specific audience, through a format that imitates a news or report (acquiring credibility), through false information that may or may not be associated with real events, with an opportunistic structure (title, image, content) to attract the readers’ attention and to persuade them to believe in falsehood, in order to obtain more clicks and shares, therefore, higher advertising revenue and/or ideological gain.”

This collection of examples serves as a stark reminder of the power and potential harm of misinformation in our digitally interconnected world. The battle against fake news remains ongoing, but with awareness and discernment, we can fortify our defenses against its deceptive influence.
